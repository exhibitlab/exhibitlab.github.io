Structures during the 1994 Northridge Earthquake

How were structures considered earthquake resistant before the earthquake?

Base Isolated Structures

The base isolated structures that are separated from the foundation with base isolators, which are made of rubber and steel and can absorb the energy released by the ground motion to reduce the structure’s vibration.

Steel Moment Frames

Steel moment frames can resist intense ground motion because of the rigidity of joints and flexibility of members which can resist the rotation at the connection.

Reinforced Concrete Shear Walls

Reinforced concrete shear walls act as a vertical diaphragm to resist lateral forces, very important in high-rise buildings.

Impact of 1994 Northridge Earthquake on structures

Base isolated structures

The base isolated structures performed well, with minor damages. The USC Hospital building reached 10% allowable drifts values, and the maximum drift was less than 30% of the code specification. However, the drift ratios will be a lot higher if the magnitude of the earthquake exceeded the 1994 earthquake’s magnitude (6.7) and may result in major damage. For instance, the Los Angeles County hospital complex resulted in high damage leading to its replacement.

Steel moment frames

The steel frames did not resist strong earthquakes since brittle fractures occurred at the beam-column connections.

Structural System

Stiff and strong structural system that was able to resist very intense shaking. The Olive View Hospital building, built in 1976 after its damage in the 1971 San Fernando Earthquake, showed no structural damage during the earthquake.

How were they improved after the earthquake?

2015 Retrofit Ordinance

Los Angeles City Council approved a mandatory retrofitting of 13,500 soft story buildings and 1,500 non-ductile reinforced concrete buildings.

Seismic Alerting System

Most improvements focused on the seismic alerting system such as the Shake Alert.

Moving the Failure

A radius cut in the beam at a specific distance from the column in order to weaken the beam, so that failure can happen in the beam instead of the connection.
