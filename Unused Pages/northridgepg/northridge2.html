Structural Impact of the 1994 Northridge Earthquake

How did these structures fare in the earthquake?

Base isolated structures

The base isolated structures performed well, with minor damages.

USC Hospital

The USC Hospital building reached the 10% allowable drifts values, and the maximum drift was less than 30% of the code specification.

Future Issues

However, the drift ratios will be a lot higher if the magnitude of the earthquake exceeded the 1994 earthquakeâ€™s magnitude (6.7) and may result in major damage. For instance, the Los Angeles County hospital complex resulted in high damage leading to its replacement.

Steel moment frames

The steel frames did not resist the strong earthquake, as shown in the brittle fractures occurring at the beam-column connections.

Structural Systems

The stiff and strong structural systems were able to resist the most intense shaking.

The Olive View Hospital

The Olive View Hospital building, re-built in 1976 due the incurred damages from the 1971 San Fernando Earthquake, showed no structural damage during the earthquake.
