CRSBF BEHAVIOR


Unlike traditional steel frames, the end columns of the Controlled Rocking Steel Brace Frame are designed to rise up when undergoing an earthquake, causing the entire frame to rotate. The post-tensioning strands in the middle of the frame provide the force needed to recenter the frame once it starts lifting off of the ground, reducing the buildingâ€™s drift.

CRSBF IMPACTS


Once the controlled rocking steel brace frame sustains damage, the cost of
the repaired and replaced parts is less
than that of a traditional steel frame. In addition, the CRSBF takes less energy to construct and emits less greenhouse gases over its lifetime. An interactive model goes in depth about how different aspects of the CRSBF affect its economic and environmental impact.

OUR THESIS

The current study seeks to evaluate the seismic performance of a 6-story controlled rocking steel braced frames (CRSBF) building and determine the optimal design parameters that will minimize the combined upfront cost (of the CRSBF only) and earthquake-induced economic losses and environmental impacts.
