RESILIENCE


Withstanding disastrous events and recovering from the damage are both vital aspects of resilience. Although an individual structure’s resilience can be considered, a disastrous event impacts far more than a single structure, so the focus should be on the entire community’s resilience.



SUSTAINABILITY


A structure’s present and future impacts on the environment, economy, and society are analyzed for sustainability. Structures should be designed to reduce impacts on both the present and the future as it goes through the routine events, such as maintenance, of its life cycle.

Control Rocking Steel Braced Frames


CRSBF Behavior

Unlike traditional steel frames, the end columns of the Controlled Rocking Steel Brace Frame are designed to rise up when undergoing an earthquake, causing the entire frame to rotate. The post-tensioning strands in the middle of the frame provide the force needed to recenter the frame once it starts lifting off of the ground, reducing the building’s drift.

CRSBF Impacts

Once the Controlled Rocking Steel Brace Frame sustains damage, the cost of the repaired and replaced parts is less than that of a traditional steel frame. In addition, the CRSBF takes less energy to construct(?) and emits less greenhouse gases over its lifetime. An interactive model goes in depth about how different aspects of the CRSBF affect its economic and environmental impact.
