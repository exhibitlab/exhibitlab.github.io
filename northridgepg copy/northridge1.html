Structural Impact of the 1994 Northridge Earthquake

How were structures considered earthquake resistant before the earthquake?

Base Isolated Structures

These structures are intentionally separated from the building’s foundation with base isolators. Base isolators, made of rubber and steel, can absorb the energy released by ground motion to reduce the structure’s vibration.

Steel Moment Frames

Steel moment frames are ideal to counteract intense ground motion, since their joints and members can resist the rotation at connections caused by earthquakes.

Rigid Joints + Flexible Members = No Rotation

Reinforced Concrete Shear Walls

Reinforced concrete shear walls act as a
vertical diaphragm to resist lateral forces, especially important in high-rise buildings.
